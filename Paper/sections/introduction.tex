\section{Introduction}
Complex dynamical systems can often be represented in multiple ways, and different representations may become useful at different prediction horizons. A critical question arises: How can we determine which representation carries the true predictive structure of the system? 

While machine learning models can discover useful representations, model-selected features often reflect the architecture, optimization method, and inductive bias of the observer rather than physical reality. Therefore, an observer-independent criterion is required. We propose the Predictive Structure Framework (PSF) to bridge this gap.
