# ICCS v1.1 Requirements

## Motivation

ICCS v1.0 proxy exposed three boundary conditions.

The goal of v1.1 is not to maximize predictive score,
but to measure structural organization under stronger invariance constraints.

---

# Requirement 1: Dynamics-aware

## Problem

Equal one-step predictive information does not imply equal dynamics.

Boundary:
Equal MI Different Dynamics

## Requirement

ICCS must distinguish systems with:

- identical short-term predictability
- different temporal organization

## Mathematical direction

ICCS should include:

$F(X_t,...,X_{t+k})$

rather than only:

$I(X_t;X_{t+1})$

Candidate components:

- multi-step predictive information
- memory decay profile
- attractor geometry
- recurrence structure

---

# Requirement 2: Representation-invariant

## Problem

Bijective transformations changed operational ICCS.

Boundary:
Adversarial Representations

## Requirement

Equivalent observers under information-preserving transformations
should converge to equivalent structural estimates.

## Mathematical direction

Need invariance under:

$X \rightarrow T(X)$

where T is bijective.

Candidate components:

- invariant embeddings
- geometric normalization
- topology-preserving descriptors
- equivalence classes of representations

---

# Requirement 3: Causal-aware

## Problem

Prediction was generated by hidden common drivers.

Boundary:
Causal vs Predictive Divergence

## Requirement

Predictive dependency must be separated from causal dependency.

## Mathematical direction

Need sensitivity to:

$P(Y_{future} | do(X))$

vs

$P(Y_{future} | X)$

Candidate components:

- temporal asymmetry
- conditional independence
- intervention proxies
- directed information measures

---

# Design Principle

ICCS v1.1 is not defined as a fixed formula yet.

It is defined by invariance requirements.

Implementation choices must satisfy the three constraints.
