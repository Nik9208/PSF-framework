# Scalar Collapse Revalidation

## Hypothesis
Does scalar projection preserve the separability observed in the multidimensional feature space $S(X)$?  
$H_0: \exists f(S),\ f \text{ preserves all tested distinctions under the Aggregation Boundary.}$

We tested three scalar projections:
1. $L_2$ Norm: $f_1(S) = ||S||_2$
2. Algebraic Sum: $f_2(S) = \sum S_i$
3. Distance to Reference: $f_3(S) = ||S - S_{ref}||$

## Observations
Based on the data in `scalar_collapse_results.csv`, the structural ordering generated by the multidimensional BVP feature space is definitively compromised when collapsed into any tested scalar projection.

While the full vector representation flawlessly preserved the relative causal gap (Gap > 0.90) between a Causal and Mimic system up to 20% relative noise, all three scalar projections allowed non-causal components (like the exceptionally high autoregressive memory $M(k)$ in the Mimic system) to compensate for the absence of causal information, reducing structural separability and effectively ranking the Mimic as "more complex".

## Conclusion
The tested scalar projections do not preserve the structural distinctions validated by the BVP suite. This empirical observation confirms the theoretical necessity of the multidimensional ICCS feature space, satisfying Boundary #4 (Aggregation Boundary) under empirical conditions.
