# Adversarial Representations

## Overview
Testing the Observer Independence Principle.

## Setup
Observer A finds $\Phi_A$.
Observer B finds $\Phi_B$.

Both achieve $I(\Phi_A; X_f) \approx I(\Phi_B; X_f)$, but the representations are drastically different:
$d(\Phi_A, \Phi_B) \gg 0$

## Failure condition
If no unique predictive attractor exists despite high prediction scores, ICCS requires refinement.
