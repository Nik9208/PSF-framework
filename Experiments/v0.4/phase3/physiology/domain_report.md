# Domain Report: Physiology (Pilot)
**Dataset:** PhysioNet NSRDB (Subject 16265)
**Date:** 2026-07-02
**Protocol:** Phase3 v1.0
**Analysis Script:** `analyze_iccs.py`
**ICCS Version:** v0.3.1 (frozen)

---

## 1. Observed Facts

1. **Dataset Integrity:** 100,183 RR-intervals were successfully extracted and filtered (bounds 0.3s - 2.0s), representing healthy normal sinus rhythm.
2. **Infrastructure:** The analysis completed successfully with no NaNs or Infs generated by the ICCS v0.3.1 core.
3. **Geometry Profile:** The mean local intrinsic dimension (`D_local`) is observed to be `0.00025` (effectively zero).
4. **Memory Profile:** The mean memory depth (`M`) is observed to be `9.06` steps.
5. **Causal Profile:** Transfer Entropy is highly asymmetric. `TE_forward` is `1.13`, while `TE_reverse` is `4.31`. Conditional Mutual Information (`CMI`) is `4.31`.

---

## 2. Interpretation Supported by Current Evidence

### Geometry (`D_local` ~ 0)
* **Interpretation:** Expected.
* **Evidence Link:** The observed near-zero `D_local` is consistent with the previously established operational boundary of the ICCS v0.3.1 Geometry component, which estimates local dimensionality directly on scalar projections without delay embedding. Within the current implementation, this limits the ability of the Geometry module to capture higher-dimensional state-space structure from univariate recordings.

### Memory (`M` = 9.06)
* **Interpretation:** Expected.
* **Evidence Link:** Healthy human heart rate variability (HRV) is widely documented in physiology to exhibit long-range fractal correlations (1/f scaling) and robust autonomic regulation memory. An `M` score of ~9 is consistent with the well-documented long-range temporal dependence observed in healthy HRV.

### Transfer Entropy / CMI Asymmetry
* **Interpretation:** Unexpected but reproducible.
* **Evidence Link:** The pronounced asymmetry (`TE_reverse` significantly higher than `TE_forward`) indicates strong directional information flow within the lagged coordinate space constructed by the causal layer. While physiological time irreversibility in healthy HRV is a known phenomenon (e.g., rapid acceleration followed by slow deceleration of heart rate), it is unclear whether this specific metric may reflect true physiological asymmetry or a mathematical artifact of the specific embedding strategy used internally within the `compute_causal_fingerprint` function. An additional possibility is that part of the observed asymmetry reflects finite-sample properties of the Transfer Entropy estimator itself rather than either physiological irreversibility or the internal lag-construction procedure.

---

## 3. Remaining Uncertainties

1. **Causal Layer Blackbox:** The internal embedding logic within the causal layer (which generated Y and Z from the univariate X) produced highly asymmetrical TE. Is this asymmetry robust across all healthy subjects in the NSRDB, or is it specific to subject 16265?
2. **Geometry Utility on Univariate Data:** Since `D_local` collapses to 0 on non-embedded 1D data, is the Geometry component of ICCS v0.3.1 fundamentally uninterpretable for strictly univariate physiological recordings without modification?
3. **Reproducibility across preprocessing:** Does the observed ICCS profile remain qualitatively unchanged under reasonable preprocessing choices that are permitted within the frozen Phase 3 protocol?
